SmartWeights

Currently designing and creating the SmartWeights application alongside a team of graduate students at Temple University. The app is designed to help users track their weightlifting progress and provide them with a personalized workout plan. The app will include a virtual pet system that provides users with rewards for completing workouts and reaching goals. The app will require users to attach sensors to their weightlifting equipment to track their progress and evaluate their form. The app is being developed in Swift and Python and will be available on the App Store. The app is currently in the design phase and all documentation can be found on the SmartWeights GitHub page and Docusaurus page:

    Created a linear regression model alongside another colleague that uses an IMDb movie database. Film attributes such as actors, genres, duration, and more were used for training the model. The linear regression models follows a 80/20 split of training and testing set. This project required many elements of data science including filling and removing missing values, examining categorical features, normalizing data using standard scalar, calculating ridge regression with different lambda values, and more.
